# Snapper

Red snapper is a lean white fish providing complete protein, moderate selenium, and modest EPA/DHA (0.33 g/100g cooked), with mercury levels well within safe limits for 2–3 servings per week.

## Why It Matters for Longevity

Snapper belongs to the Longevity Diet's rotating fish protocol — low-mercury lean white fish that contribute to weekly seafood variety without the mercury load of larger predatory fish.

While snapper's omega-3 content is modest compared to oily fish, fish consumption broadly reduces cardiovascular mortality even at modest EPA/DHA levels. Jayedi et al.'s (2018) dose-response meta-analysis of 14 prospective cohort studies (911,348 participants) found that each 20 g/day increment in fish consumption was significantly associated with lower cardiovascular and all-cause mortality — with benefits evident even from lean white fish.

Mozaffarian & Rimm's comprehensive 2006 review confirmed the cardiovascular benefit of regular fish consumption outweighs mercury risks for low-mercury species, and snapper's average mercury content (~0.17 ppm) places it in the low-risk tier.

Snapper's selenium content (38 mcg/100g) is also meaningful — organic selenomethionine from fish is highly bioavailable and supports antioxidant enzyme systems (glutathione peroxidase) critical to longevity biology.

## How to Use It

Pairs well with lemon, olive oil, tomatoes. Bake or pan-sear; mild flavor accepts bold Mediterranean preparations (olives, capers, cherry tomatoes). Works well whole or as fillets.

## Synergies

- **Sardines** (complement): Book recommends 2–3 fish servings/week; snapper as lean white fish complements high-omega-3 oily fish like sardines.
- **Olive Oil** (synergy): Adds MUFA and improves absorption of fat-soluble vitamins; traditional Mediterranean preparation.
- **Lemon** (complement): Acid brightens the mild flavor and adds vitamin C to the meal.

## Flavor Profile

Taste: mild, slightly sweet, nutty. Aroma: fresh, oceanic. Texture: firm, flaky, moist. Category: white fish.

## The Science

- [Jayedi et al., 2018, *Crit Rev Food Sci Nutr*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29317009/): Dose-response meta-analysis (14 studies, 911,348 participants): each 20 g/day fish increment inversely associated with cardiovascular and all-cause mortality — benefits extend to lean white fish.
- [Mozaffarian & Rimm, 2006, *JAMA*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/17047219/): Benefits of fish consumption for cardiovascular mortality substantially outweigh methylmercury risks for low-mercury species like snapper (~0.17 ppm average).
