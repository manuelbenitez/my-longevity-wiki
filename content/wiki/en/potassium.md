# Potassium

50–90% of US adults are deficient in potassium, making it one of the most widespread nutritional gaps. The Longevity Diet addresses this through adequate intake via a comprehensive multivitamin every 2–3 days and regular consumption of legumes, vegetables, and fish. Potassium is essential for fluid balance, nerve impulse transmission, muscle contraction, and blood pressure regulation via the renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system.

## Why It Matters for Longevity

Potassium is a critical determinant of cardiovascular longevity: it counteracts sodium's hypertensive effects, relaxes arterial walls, and reduces stroke risk. Most Western diets provide insufficient potassium relative to sodium, reversing the favorable potassium:sodium ratio seen in traditional plant-based eating patterns.

A meta-analysis of 22 randomized controlled trials found that increased potassium intake significantly reduced systolic blood pressure by 3.49 mmHg and was associated with 24% lower stroke risk — establishing a direct cardiovascular longevity benefit ([Aburto et al., 2013, *BMJ*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23558164/)).

A dose-response meta-analysis of RCTs confirmed that potassium intake between 90–120 mmol/day produces the greatest blood pressure reductions, with effects most pronounced in hypertensive individuals and those with high sodium intake ([Filippini et al., 2020, *J Am Heart Assoc*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32500831/)).

## How to Use It

Prioritize high-potassium foods: white beans (~1,000 mg/100 g dry), baked potato with skin (~544 mg/100 g), cooked spinach (~558 mg/100 g), avocado, lentils, and salmon. The Longevity Diet's legume-heavy structure naturally delivers high potassium intakes without supplementation.
