# Fennel

Vegetable used in the Longevity Diet fennel salad and as a side dish ingredient. Provides dietary fiber, vitamin C, potassium, and bioactive phytochemicals including anethole and fenchone as part of a plant-forward longevity eating pattern.

## Why It Matters for Longevity

Fennel (*Foeniculum vulgare*) is an aromatic Mediterranean vegetable whose health profile extends beyond basic nutrition. Its principal volatile compounds — particularly anethole and fenchone — have documented anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties that may contribute to reduced chronic disease risk within the context of a Mediterranean diet.

Essential oils of *Foeniculum vulgare* demonstrated significant in vitro anti-arthritic activity by inhibiting protein denaturation and membrane stabilization — mechanisms underlying chronic inflammatory joint disease — establishing a mechanistic basis for the anti-inflammatory effects attributed to fennel ([Marrelli et al., 2020, *Chem Biodivers*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33063941/)).

Fenchone, a major compound in fennel, ameliorated experimental colitis in rats via antioxidant and immunomodulatory mechanisms, significantly reducing inflammatory biomarkers — supporting inclusion of fennel in anti-inflammatory dietary patterns ([Araruna et al., 2024, *Pharmaceuticals*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39861081/)).

## How to Use It

Slice bulb thinly for raw fennel salad with lemon and olive oil (the Longevity Diet preparation). Roast wedges at high heat with olive oil and orange. Add to fish dishes; fennel pairs naturally with sardines and anchovies in traditional Italian cooking. Use fronds as an herb garnish.

## Flavor Profile

Mildly sweet, anise-like, subtly licorice. Aroma is fresh, anise, herbaceous. Texture is crisp raw, tender when cooked. The fronds have a more intense anise flavor than the bulb. Cooking mellows the licorice note considerably.

## The Science

- [Marrelli et al., 2020, *Chem Biodivers*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33063941/): Essential oils of *Foeniculum vulgare* subsp. *piperitum* demonstrated significant in vitro anti-arthritic activity via protein denaturation inhibition and membrane stabilization mechanisms.
- [Araruna et al., 2024, *Pharmaceuticals*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39861081/): Fenchone, a major fennel compound, ameliorated TNBS-induced colitis in rats via antioxidant and immunomodulatory pathways, reducing pro-inflammatory cytokines and oxidative stress markers.
