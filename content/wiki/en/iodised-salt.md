# Iodised Salt

A double-edged ingredient: 29% of the world's population remains iodine-insufficient, yet excess sodium drives hypertension and bone loss. The goal is enough iodine from as little salt as possible.

## Why It Matters for Longevity

Iodine is the rate-limiting substrate for thyroid hormones T3 and T4, which regulate metabolism, body temperature, and -- critically during pregnancy -- brain development. Mild iodine deficiency in pregnant and breastfeeding women impairs neuronal migration, myelination, and synaptogenesis in the developing brain, potentially reducing IQ and increasing ADHD risk.

A comprehensive review found that 29% of the global population remains iodine-insufficient, including populations in developed nations; iodised salt remains the most effective global public health intervention for preventing iodine deficiency disorders, including thyroid enlargement, hypothyroidism, and neurocognitive impairment ([Zimmermann and Boelaert, 2015, *Lancet Diabetes Endocrinol*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25591468/)).

The tension is real: reducing salt to <5g/day (WHO target) lowers blood pressure and cardiovascular events, but eliminating salt entirely risks iodine deficiency unless alternative sources are secured. Each 2.3g of sodium consumed increases urinary calcium excretion by approximately 40mg, contributing to bone loss -- particularly problematic for postmenopausal women already at osteoporosis risk.

The solution: use iodised salt as your sole home salt source (~5g/day provides 150mcg iodine, 100% RDA), supplement with iodine-rich foods (dried seaweed: 65-680mcg/100g; marine seafood: 50-100mcg/100g), and use herbs, spices, garlic, and lemon juice to add flavor complexity that reduces dependence on salt quantity.

## How to Use It

~5g iodised salt per day as your sole salt source. If reducing salt below this, supplement iodine from seaweed, seafood, or small amounts of dairy. Store sealed to prevent iodine loss from humidity. Use herbs, spices, and acid (lemon, vinegar) to reduce salt needs.

## Flavor Profile

Pure salinity with mineral notes. Iodised salt has a faintly metallic aftertaste that some detect. Fine-grain dissolves instantly; coarse provides finishing texture. The flavor contribution is enhancement of other ingredients rather than its own distinct taste.

## The Science

- [Zimmermann and Boelaert, 2015, *Lancet Diabetes Endocrinol*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25591468/): 29% of global population iodine-insufficient; iodised salt is the most effective intervention for preventing iodine deficiency disorders including hypothyroidism, goitre, and neurocognitive impairment across the life course.
