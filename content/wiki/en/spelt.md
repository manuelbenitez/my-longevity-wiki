# Spelt

Whole ancient grain used as pasta substitute and grain base in the Longevity Diet — acceptable alongside farro, barley, and whole wheat pasta at 40g dry weight per serving.

## Why It Matters for Longevity

Spelt is an ancient wheat variety (Triticum aestivum ssp. spelta) with a modestly superior nutritional profile to modern bread wheat: higher magnesium (136 mg/100g vs ~90 mg), zinc, and B vitamins, with a more water-soluble gluten structure.

The longevity case for spelt rests primarily on its whole-grain status. Aune et al.'s landmark 2016 BMJ meta-analysis of 45 studies found that whole grain consumption is dose-dependently associated with lower risk of coronary heart disease (−19% per 90 g/day), cardiovascular disease, total cancer, and all-cause mortality. Spelt consumed whole — as grain, spelt pasta, or spelt bread — delivers the same arabinoxylan prebiotic fiber, lignans, and phytonutrients responsible for these associations.

Refined spelt provides no meaningful advantage over refined wheat. The key is the whole grain matrix.

## How to Use It

Pairs well with legumes, olive oil, vegetables. Cook spelt grain like rice (1:2 water ratio, ~45 minutes) or use as a pasta substitute. Spelt pasta is widely available and has a nuttier flavor than wheat pasta that holds up well to olive oil-based sauces.

## Synergies

- **Legumes** (complement): Ancient grain + legume combination provides complete amino acid profile; classic Mediterranean pairing.
- **Olive Oil** (synergy): Fat lowers glycemic load of spelt dishes; traditional dressing in grain salads.
- **Pasta** (complement): Book lists spelt as an interchangeable 40g dry-weight substitute for pasta in longevity meal plans.

## Flavor Profile

Taste: nutty, earthy, slightly sweet. Aroma: toasty, wheaty. Texture: chewy, dense. Category: ancient grain.

## The Science

- [Aune et al., 2016, *BMJ*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27301975/): Dose-response meta-analysis of 45 studies: whole grain consumption (90 g/day) associated with 19% lower coronary heart disease risk, 22% lower CVD risk, and 17% lower all-cause mortality.
- [Galterio et al., 2005, *Plant Foods Hum Nutr*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15796621/): Spelt bran is naturally enriched in minerals and oleic acid; calcium, magnesium, and zinc are higher than in common wheat flour with comparable phytic acid levels.
