# Blueberry Jam

No-sugar-added blueberry jam is used as a breakfast spread in the Longevity Diet, preserving most of the polyphenol content of whole blueberries while fitting the low-refined-sugar dietary pattern.

## Why It Matters for Longevity

Blueberries are one of the most polyphenol-dense fruits in the Western diet. When made into jam, a portion of the anthocyanins is retained -- particularly when no sugar is added, as high sugar concentrations accelerate polyphenol degradation during processing.

Jam processing and storage significantly affects blueberry polyphenolics: while anthocyanin content decreases with heating, total antioxidant capacity is better retained in no-added-sugar preparations ([Howard et al., 2010, *J Agric Food Chem*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20055410/)).

In participants with metabolic syndrome, 6-week daily blueberry consumption improved endothelial function, reduced vascular stiffness, and improved circulating angiogenic cell counts -- demonstrating meaningful cardiovascular benefit from blueberry-derived bioactives ([Curtis et al., 2019, *Am J Clin Nutr*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31136659/)).

## How to Use It

Spread on whole-grain bread or toast, or stir into plain goat's milk yogurt. Choose jams with no added sugar -- the label should list only blueberries (and possibly pectin). The pectin in jam provides additional soluble fiber with prebiotic properties.

## Flavor Profile

Sweet, tart, and intensely fruity. Aroma is warm and berry-like. Texture is smooth and slightly gel-like from pectin. No-sugar-added versions are noticeably less sweet and more tart than commercial preparations, with more prominent blueberry flavour.

## The Science

- [Howard et al., 2010, *J Agric Food Chem*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20055410/): Jam processing and storage significantly affects blueberry polyphenolics; no-added-sugar preparations retain antioxidant capacity better than high-sugar formulations.
- [Curtis et al., 2019, *Am J Clin Nutr*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31136659/): Daily blueberry consumption for 6 weeks improved endothelial function, reduced vascular stiffness, and improved angiogenic cell counts in participants with metabolic syndrome.
