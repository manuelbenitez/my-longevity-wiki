# Dark Green Leafy Vegetables

One serving of green leafy vegetables per day slowed cognitive decline by the equivalent of 11 years in the MIND study. That is not a supplement -- that is a daily salad or a side of sauteed greens.

## Why It Matters for Longevity

Dark leafy greens sit at the intersection of nearly every longevity-relevant nutrient pathway. They provide vitamin K1 (a single serving of kale delivers 482% of the RDA), which is essential for both blood clotting and directing calcium into bones rather than arteries. They are among the richest dietary sources of lutein and zeaxanthin, carotenoids that accumulate in the retinal macula and protect against age-related macular degeneration -- the AREDS2 trial (Ma et al., 2012, PMID 22170359) showed these compounds reduced disease progression. And they provide folate, the natural form of folic acid, which is critical for DNA methylation and was shown to reduce precancerous colon lesion recurrence three-fold in a small RCT.

The cognitive benefits are particularly striking. Morris et al. (2018, PMID 29263222) followed 960 older adults and found that those eating roughly one serving of leafy greens daily had significantly slower rates of cognitive decline. The effect was large enough to represent an 11-year difference in brain aging. The likely mechanisms include folate's role in homocysteine metabolism, vitamin K's involvement in sphingolipid synthesis in the brain, and nitrate's effect on cerebral blood flow.

Leafy greens are also major sources of dietary nitrate, which oral bacteria convert to nitric oxide -- a molecule that dilates blood vessels and lowers blood pressure. Wang et al. (2016, PMID 26946249) found a 15.8% reduction in cardiovascular disease risk with higher dietary nitrate from greens. One important caveat: antiseptic mouthwash kills the oral bacteria responsible for this conversion, potentially negating the cardiovascular benefit.

## How to Use It

Eat at every meal -- raw in salads, lightly steamed, or sauteed with garlic and olive oil. Avoid boiling, which strips water-soluble vitamins and minerals. Pair with fat (olive oil, nuts, avocado) to absorb fat-soluble vitamins K and A and carotenoids. Rotate varieties: spinach, kale, silver beet, beet greens, endive, chicory, dandelion greens, mustard greens. Each provides a different phytochemical profile. Do not use antibacterial mouthwash if you want the nitrate-to-nitric-oxide conversion.

## Flavor Profile

Ranges from mild and tender (baby spinach, lettuce) to assertively bitter and mineral (mature kale, dandelion greens). Young leaves are sweeter; mature leaves develop deeper bitterness and more robust texture. Cooking tames bitterness and concentrates flavor. The grassy, chlorophyll-rich aroma is distinctive and signals nutrient density.

## The Science

- Morris et al. (2018): One daily serving slowed cognitive decline equivalent to 11 years younger (PMID 29263222)
- Wang et al. (2016): Higher dietary nitrate from greens linked to 15.8% lower CVD risk (PMID 26946249)
- Ma et al. (2012): Lutein/zeaxanthin reduced macular degeneration progression in AREDS2 trial (PMID 22170359)
- Examine.com: Vitamin K1 deficiency linked to arterial calcification and osteoporosis; most Western diets are suboptimal
