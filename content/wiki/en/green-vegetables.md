# Green Vegetables

Green vegetables have an almost undetectable effect on blood glucose -- very low carbohydrate, very high fiber. Among all vegetable subgroups, greens show the strongest dose-response relationship with reduced mortality.

## Why It Matters for Longevity

Increased green vegetable intake is associated with 14% reduced risk of type 2 diabetes per 1.15 servings per day ([Carter et al., 2010, *BMJ*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20724400/)). Cruciferous and green leafy vegetable intake specifically reduces subclinical atherosclerosis in older adults — an effect not seen with other vegetable types, confirming that greens provide unique cardiovascular benefits beyond general vegetable intake ([Blekkenhorst et al., 2018, *J Am Heart Assoc*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29618474/)). Chlorophyll, the pigment responsible for the green color, contains magnesium at its center and may have anti-mutagenic properties.

## How to Use It

Eat green vegetables daily with every main meal. Dress with extra-virgin olive oil to improve fat-soluble nutrient absorption. Brief cooking (steaming, blanching) preserves more nutrients than prolonged boiling. Eat a variety: dark leafy greens (spinach, kale), cruciferous (broccoli, kohlrabi), and aromatic greens (fennel, celery).

## Flavor Profile

Highly variable: from delicate butter lettuce to peppery arugula, bitter escarole to mild spinach, crisp fennel to earthy kale. The bitter notes in darker greens (from glucosinolates, sesquiterpene lactones) diminish with brief cooking. Color intensity reflects polyphenol and chlorophyll concentration.

## The Science

- [Carter et al., 2010, *BMJ*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20724400/): Meta-analysis found 14% reduced risk of type 2 diabetes per 1.15 servings/day of green leafy vegetables.
- [Blekkenhorst et al., 2018, *J Am Heart Assoc*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29618474/): Cruciferous and total vegetable intake inversely associated with subclinical atherosclerosis progression in older adults, with cruciferous vegetables showing strongest association.
