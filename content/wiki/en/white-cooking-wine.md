# White Cooking Wine

Used in small amounts for cooking shellfish in the Longevity Diet.

## Why It Matters for Longevity

Used in small amounts for cooking shellfish in the Longevity Diet.  White wine used in cooking retains polyphenols (tyrosol, caffeic acid) even after heating; alcohol content largely evaporates within 15 minutes of cooking. (Food Chemistry, 2016 (PMID 27059772))  Small amounts of white wine in cooking contribute negligible alcohol to the final dish and provide volatile aromatic compounds that enhance palatability. (European Journal of Clinical Nutrition, 2004 (PMID 15164105))

## How to Use It

Pairs well with shellfish (clams, mussels), garlic, shallots. Use as a beverage in your daily meals according to the Longevity Diet guidelines.

## Synergies

- **Shellfish** (complement): Acidity of wine denatures shellfish proteins improving texture; enhances absorption of shellfish zinc and selenium  - **Garlic** (synergy): Allicin from garlic and tyrosol from wine together inhibit platelet aggregation and reduce cardiovascular risk  - **Olive Oil** (complement): Wine acids and olive oil polyphenols form an emulsion improving flavor integration and phytonutrient delivery

## Flavor Profile

Taste: acidic, fruity, dry. Aroma: floral, fruity, yeasty. Texture: liquid. Category: cooking liquid / flavor base.
