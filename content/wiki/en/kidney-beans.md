# Kidney Beans

Source of folate (46 mcg per ½ cup canned, 12% DV), iron (2 mg per ½ cup canned, 11% DV), and magnesium (35 mg per ½ cup canned, 9% DV).

## Why It Matters for Longevity

Source of folate (46 mcg per ½ cup canned, 12% DV), iron (2 mg per ½ cup canned, 11% DV), and magnesium (35 mg per ½ cup canned, 9% DV). Plant protein source providing multiple micronutrients (folate for DNA synthesis, iron for immune function, magnesium for enzymatic reactions) critical for longevity; legumes are a cornerstone of all Blue Zone and centenarian diets..  Legume consumption (including kidney beans) is the single most consistent dietary predictor of longevity across five Blue Zone centenarian populations; each 20 g/day increase associated with a 7–8% reduction in mortality risk. (PMID 24871476) (PubMed)  Kidney beans contain resistant starch and soluble fiber that act as prebiotics, promoting short-chain fatty acid production (butyrate), reducing gut inflammation, and supporting microbiome diversity linked to healthy aging. (PMID 26853820) (PubMed)

## How to Use It

Pairs well with olive oil, lemon, lettuce. Use as a legume in your daily meals according to the Longevity Diet guidelines.

## Synergies

- **Lemon** (synergy): Vitamin C from lemon dramatically enhances non-heme iron absorption from kidney beans — the key strategy for iron adequacy on plant-based longevity diets  - **Olive Oil** (complement): Olive oil enhances satiety and micronutrient absorption; the fat-soluble carotenoids present in bean dishes are better absorbed with olive oil  - **Lettuce** (complement): Classic longevity salad pairing in The Longevity Diet; provides complementary textures and nutrients

## Flavor Profile

Taste: earthy, slightly nutty, mild, creamy when cooked. Aroma: neutral earthy, subtle legume aroma. Texture: firm-tender, mealy interior, skin holds shape when cooked. Category: legume / pulse.

## Key Nutrients

| Nutrient | Per 100g | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| Dietary fiber | ~7.4 g (cooked) | High in both soluble fiber (cholesterol-lowering) and resistant starch (prebiotic); fermented in colon to produce butyrate |
| Plant protein | ~8.7 g (cooked) | Lower IGF-1 stimulation than animal protein; digestibility enhanced by cooking and soaking |
| Folate | ~33 mcg (cooked) | Folate is heat-sensitive; canned beans retain approximately 60–70% of original folate content |
| Iron (non-heme) | ~2.9 mg (cooked) | Absorption enhanced by vitamin C co-ingestion; inhibited by phytates in same meal |
| Magnesium | ~45 mg (cooked) | Bioavailability ~30–40%; soaking reduces phytate inhibition |
