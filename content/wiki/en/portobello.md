# Portobello

Portobello is included in the Longevity Diet as part of the mushroom group (75–150 g raw or boiled per dish). UV-exposed portobello can generate 400–870 IU of bioavailable vitamin D2 per 100 g, and contains ergothioneine and beta-glucans associated with immune modulation and longevity.

## Why It Matters for Longevity

Portobello mushrooms are a practical source of vitamin D2 (ergocalciferol) when exposed to UV light — placing them gills-side up in midday sun for 30–60 minutes generates meaningful amounts that raise serum 25(OH)D comparably to supplemental D2. Mushrooms contain ergosterol in their cell walls, which converts to ergocalciferol upon UV exposure; dietary vitamin D supports immune function, bone health, and modulates aging-related inflammatory pathways.

UV-B irradiated button mushrooms significantly increased serum 25(OH)D in vitamin D-deficient adults, confirming that UV-exposed mushrooms are a bioavailable dietary source of vitamin D2 equivalent to supplemental vitamin D2 ([Urbain et al., 2011, *Eur J Clin Nutr*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21540874/)).

Medicinal mushrooms including portobello contain high-molecular-weight beta-glucan polysaccharides that stimulate innate immune cells via Dectin-1 and Toll-like receptors, demonstrating immunomodulating and anti-tumor activity in laboratory and clinical studies ([Wasser, 2002, *Appl Microbiol Biotechnol*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12436306/)).

## How to Use It

Use 75–150 g per dish according to the Longevity Diet guidelines. For vitamin D, place portobello gills-side up in midday sun for 30–60 minutes before cooking — this dramatically increases D2 content. Grill, roast, or sauté with olive oil and garlic. Works well as a meat substitute in pasta and grain dishes.

## Flavor Profile

Umami-rich, meaty, earthy, and savory. Aroma intensifies when grilled or roasted — forest floor with caramelized depth. Texture is dense and meaty, absorbs marinades well, tender when cooked.

## The Science

- [Urbain et al., 2011, *Eur J Clin Nutr*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21540874/): UV-B irradiated button mushrooms significantly increased serum 25(OH)D in vitamin D-deficient adults, confirming bioavailability of mushroom vitamin D2 comparable to supplemental vitamin D2.
- [Wasser, 2002, *Appl Microbiol Biotechnol*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12436306/): Medicinal mushrooms contain immunomodulating and anti-tumor beta-glucan polysaccharides that activate innate immune cells via pattern-recognition receptors.
