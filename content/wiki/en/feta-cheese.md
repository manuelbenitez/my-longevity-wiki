# Feta Cheese

Feta (preferably from goat's or sheep's milk) is one of the few animal-derived foods recommended in the Longevity Diet, especially for those over 65. Used in small amounts in the grilled eggplant dish and Greek salad — about 20 g per dish — as a source of calcium and flavor.

## Why It Matters for Longevity

Goat and sheep milk cheeses have a distinct fatty acid composition compared to cow milk products: higher proportions of medium-chain fatty acids and conjugated linoleic acid (CLA), lower αs1-casein (potentially reducing allergenicity), and smaller fat globules that may improve digestibility. Feta's fermentation process reduces lactose content and produces bioactive peptides with ACE-inhibitory properties.

Hard and semi-hard cheeses from cow, sheep, and goat milk differ significantly in fatty acid composition; sheep and goat cheeses contain more medium-chain fatty acids and omega-3 PUFAs than cow cheese, with lipid quality indices (atherogenicity, thrombogenicity) generally more favorable in small-ruminant cheeses ([Paszczyk et al., 2020, *Foods*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33203107/)).

Fermented dairy products including cheese provide probiotic cultures, bioactive peptides, and vitamin K2 that support cardiometabolic health; prospective cohort data consistently show fermented dairy intake associated with reduced cardiovascular disease risk and all-cause mortality ([Companys et al., 2020, *Adv Nutr*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32277831/)).

## How to Use It

Use 20 g crumbled on grilled eggplant or in Greek salad as in the Longevity Diet prescriptions. Pair with tomatoes, olives, and olive oil. Choose authentic Greek feta (PDO) made from sheep's milk or a blend with goat's milk for the best fatty acid profile.

## Flavor Profile

Tangy, salty, milky, slightly acidic, creamy. Aroma is milky, lactic, slightly barn-like in sheep/goat varieties. Texture is crumbly, creamy, dense. Traditional Greek feta aged in brine develops sharper, more complex flavor than mass-produced varieties.

## The Science

- [Paszczyk et al., 2020, *Foods*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33203107/): Comparison of fatty acid composition in hard cow, sheep, and goat cheese found sheep and goat varieties contain more medium-chain fatty acids and omega-3 PUFAs, with more favorable lipid quality indices.
- [Companys et al., 2020, *Adv Nutr*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32277831/): Systematic review found fermented dairy associated with reduced cardiometabolic disease risk; fermentation generates bioactive peptides and delivers probiotic bacteria supporting metabolic health.
