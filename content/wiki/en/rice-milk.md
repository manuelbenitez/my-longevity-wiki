# Rice Milk

Listed as a plant milk providing vitamin D (101 IU per 8 fl oz unsweetened, 25.25% DV) when fortified. The Longevity Diet uses plant milks as dairy alternatives for those avoiding cow's milk. Rice milk has the mildest allergen profile of all plant milks, making it an option for those with soy, nut, or oat sensitivities.

## Why It Matters for Longevity

As a plant-based dairy alternative, rice milk allows the Longevity Diet pattern to be followed without relying on cow's dairy. When fortified, it can deliver meaningful vitamin D and calcium — key nutrients for bone health and immune function. Its main limitation versus other plant milks is lower protein content and a higher glycemic index.

Plant-based milks including rice milk can effectively deliver vitamin D and calcium when fortified, and selecting the right plant milk type depends on nutritional priorities and sustainability considerations ([Ramsing et al., 2023, *Curr Environ Health Rep*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37300651/)).

A nutritional assessment of plant-based beverages found that fortified versions can match dairy milk for key micronutrients including calcium and vitamin D, though protein content and overall nutritional profiles vary significantly by type ([Smith et al., 2022, *Front Nutr*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36003838/)).

## How to Use It

Use in place of dairy milk in coffee, tea, oatmeal, and baked goods. Choose fortified, unsweetened versions. Be aware of higher carbohydrate content (~9 g/100 g) compared to other plant milks — relevant for those managing blood sugar. Note that rice naturally concentrates inorganic arsenic, and high daily consumption is not recommended, particularly for children.
