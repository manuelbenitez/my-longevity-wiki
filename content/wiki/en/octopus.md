## Why It Matters for Longevity

Octopus is a Longevity Diet seafood featured in the traditional dish polpo e patate. It provides a small but meaningful amount of omega-3 DHA (approximately 0.13 g per 75 g cooked) and is a lean protein source. Low-fat, high-protein marine food with modest omega-3 contribution; supports muscle maintenance without the saturated fat burden of land animal proteins..  Cephalopods including octopus are consumed regularly in Mediterranean Blue Zone regions and contribute to the characteristic high seafood intake pattern associated with reduced cardiovascular and all-cause mortality. (Simopoulos, Nutrients (2011) — PMID 22254109)  Octopus provides taurine (a conditionally essential amino acid), zinc, selenium, and vitamin B12 in meaningful amounts; taurine has anti-aging properties demonstrated in animal models and epidemiological studies. (Ozogul & Ozogul, Food Chemistry (2007) — PMID unavailable; DOI 10.1016/j.foodchem.2006.01.002)

## Synergies

- **Potatoes** (complement): Traditional polpo e patate pairing provides complete amino acids, complex carbohydrates, potassium, and omega-3 in a single dish aligned with Blue Zone Calabrian cuisine.  - **Extra-Virgin-Olive-Oil** (complement): Olive oil's monounsaturated fats and polyphenols complement octopus's lean protein and trace omega-3, forming a heart-protective combination.  - **Parsley** (complement): Parsley vitamin C enhances iron absorption from octopus; the pairing is a culinary classic in Mediterranean seafood cooking.

## Flavor Profile

Taste: mild, slightly sweet, umami. Aroma: oceanic, neutral, faintly briny. Texture: tender when slow-cooked, chewy if undercooked, firm. Category: seafood / cephalopod.
