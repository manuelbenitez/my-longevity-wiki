# Goat's Milk Yogurt

Recommended in the Longevity Diet for those over 65 losing muscle mass, and consumed in high-longevity regions including Sardinia and southern Italy. Goat's milk proteins and probiotics support gut health; its different protein and fat structure may improve digestibility compared to cow's milk.

## Why It Matters for Longevity

Goat's milk contains smaller fat globules, higher medium-chain triglycerides (MCTs), and lower αs1-casein than cow's milk. These properties may reduce allergenicity and improve digestibility. Traditional consumption in Blue Zone populations supports inclusion in longevity-aligned dietary patterns.

Probiotic fermented goats' milk significantly reduced oxidative stress markers and improved antioxidant status in a clinical study, suggesting cardiovascular-protective effects from the combination of goat milk nutrients and probiotic cultures ([Kullisaar et al., 2003, *Br J Nutr*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12908907/)).

Goat cheese naturally rich in omega-3 and conjugated linoleic acid (CLA) improved cardiometabolic biomarkers including triglycerides, HDL cholesterol, and inflammatory markers in a randomized trial, indicating the distinct fatty acid composition of goat's milk products confers measurable health benefits ([Santurino et al., 2020, *Nutrients*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32380746/)).

## How to Use It

Eat as a snack with walnuts, honey, or berries as in the Longevity Diet protocol. Use plain (no added sugar) goat yogurt for maximum probiotic benefit. Pair with blueberry jam or fresh fruit. Recommended serving in the Longevity Diet protocol is 125 g.

## Flavor Profile

Tangy, slightly earthy, mildly sweet. Aroma is grassy, slightly musky — characteristic of goat's milk. Texture is creamy, slightly grainy. Distinctly different from cow milk yogurt; the flavor is more pronounced and complex.

## The Science

- [Kullisaar et al., 2003, *Br J Nutr*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12908907/): Probiotic fermented goats' milk significantly reduced oxidative stress-mediated atherogenesis markers and improved antioxidant status in clinical study.
- [Santurino et al., 2020, *Nutrients*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32380746/): Consumption of goat cheese naturally rich in omega-3 and CLA improved cardiometabolic biomarkers including lipid profile and inflammatory markers in a randomized trial.
