# Beans & Legumes

Legume consumption is the single most consistent dietary predictor of longevity across cultures. An 8% reduction in mortality risk per 20g daily intake, across every population studied.

## Why It Matters for Longevity

Beans prevent belly fat accumulation and chronic disease through fiber-rich metabolites produced by gut bacteria. A meta-analysis of 21 RCTs found one daily serving reduces LDL cholesterol by 5%. Another meta-analysis showed pulses increase satiety by 31% compared to control diets. The fiber and resistant starch feed beneficial gut bacteria, producing butyrate and other short-chain fatty acids. Cooling cooked beans increases their resistant starch content further.

This is an overview page. For specific legumes, see:
- [Lentils](/wiki/lentils) -- fastest cooking, highest polyphenols
- [Chickpeas](/wiki/chickpeas) -- the hummus legume, excellent satiety
- Soybeans -- isoflavones and complete protein

## How to Use It

Dried beans are cheap and keep indefinitely. Canned beans are nutritionally equivalent for most purposes. Fresh shell beans (fava, cranberry, lima) appear in summer. Aim for at least one serving daily -- the dose that shows consistent benefit in trials.

## What to Pair It With

| Ingredient | Why | Tradition |
|---|---|---|
| Whole grains | Complementary amino acids for complete protein | Global (rice and beans) |
| Garlic & onions | Foundational aromatics for every bean dish on earth | Global |
| Tomatoes | Acid, umami, and vitamin C for iron absorption | Mediterranean / Mexican |
| Extra-virgin olive oil | Finishing fat; carries flavor and adds monounsaturated fats | Mediterranean |
| Lemon juice | Vitamin C dramatically improves non-heme iron absorption | Mediterranean |

## Key Nutrients

| Nutrient | Per 100g (cooked) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Fiber | 5-9 g (varies by variety) | Soluble + insoluble + resistant starch; feeds gut bacteria |
| Protein | 7-10 g | High in lysine, low in methionine; complement with grains |
| Folate (B9) | 100-180 mcg | Excellent source; essential for DNA methylation |
| Potassium | 350-500 mg | Most adults are deficient; beans are a top source |
| Resistant starch | 1-4 g (increases with cooling) | Prebiotic; content rises when cooked then cooled |
