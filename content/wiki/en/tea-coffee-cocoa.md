# Tea, Coffee & Cocoa

Three of the most polyphenol-rich beverages on earth — and all three block iron absorption when consumed with meals. This page covers their shared interaction with mineral absorption. For the individual longevity profiles, see the dedicated pages.

## The Meal Timing Rule

Polyphenols (tannins) in tea, coffee, and cocoa chelate non-heme iron in the gut, forming insoluble complexes your body cannot absorb. Black tea reduces iron absorption by 79–94%. Even herbal teas and cocoa show significant inhibition. Hurrell et al. (1999) tested 16 beverages and found all polyphenol-containing drinks were potent inhibitors: those with 20–50 mg polyphenols reduced absorption 50–70%; those with 100–400 mg reduced it 60–90%.

The fix is simple: drink these beverages between meals, not during them. Thankachan et al. (2008) showed that the interaction of tea with iron absorption depends on iron status — and the practical recommendation is to wait 1 hour after meals before consuming tea or coffee, which largely eliminates the inhibitory effect.

If you must drink tea or coffee with a meal, vitamin C partially counteracts the effect — add lemon, or include bell peppers or fresh chili in the meal.

## Key Interaction

| Beverage | Tannin content | Iron absorption reduction |
|---|---|---|
| Black tea | ~4.4% | 79–94% |
| Green tea | ~3% | 20–70% |
| Coffee | ~1–2% | 20–73% |
| Cocoa | ~6% | Significant (dose-dependent) |

## The Science

- [Hurrell et al., 1999, *Br J Nutr*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10999016/): All polyphenol-rich beverages tested were potent inhibitors of non-heme iron absorption; black tea reduced absorption by 79–94%, herbal teas by 52–73%, cocoa by 71%.
- [Thankachan et al., 2008, *Am J Clin Nutr*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18400710/): Tea inhibits iron absorption from rice meals in young Indian women; ascorbic acid partially counteracts the effect; timing tea between meals preserves iron bioavailability.
