# Oily Fish

Broiled or baked oily fish three times a week cuts heart attack death risk by 49% and sudden cardiac death risk by 58% -- but frying in hydrogenated oil erases every benefit.

## Why It Matters for Longevity

The Cardiovascular Health Study delivered one of the starkest findings in nutrition research: preparation method determines whether fish saves your life or does nothing. Broiled and baked oily fish at 3+ servings per week produced a 49% reduction in heart attack death and 58% reduction in sudden cardiac death. Commercially fried fish, cooked in partially hydrogenated oils containing trans fats, showed zero protection.

The mechanisms are well-mapped. EPA and DHA stabilize cardiac cell membranes, blocking the erratic electrical impulses that trigger fatal arrhythmias. They lower triglycerides, decrease blood pressure, reduce platelet aggregation, and improve arterial function. The REDUCE-IT trial (Bhatt et al., 2019) confirmed this with pharmaceutical-grade EPA, showing a 25% reduction in major cardiovascular events. A prospective cohort of 420,000 participants (Li et al., 2021) found 2+ servings per week of oily fish lowered sudden cardiac death risk by 17%.

Cold-water species concentrate the most omega-3: salmon (1,200-2,400mg EPA+DHA per serving), anchovies (2,300-2,400mg), mackerel (1,350-2,100mg), sardines (1,100-1,600mg). These small, short-lived species also accumulate far less mercury than large predators like swordfish or shark. A Cochrane review of 86 RCTs (Abdelhamid et al., 2020) found moderate evidence that increasing EPA/DHA reduces coronary events, with stronger effects at higher doses.

## How to Use It

Two to three servings per week, broiled, baked, or grilled. Never deep-fried. Choose salmon, sardines, anchovies, mackerel, or herring. Canned sardines in olive oil retain full omega-3 content. Cold-water species are preferred for higher omega-3 density.

## Flavor Profile

Rich, savory, and unmistakably oceanic. Salmon is buttery and mild; mackerel and sardines are more assertive with deeper umami. Grilling or smoking adds complexity. The texture is moist and flaky with an oily richness that distinguishes these from lean white fish. Fresh sardines grilled over charcoal are a different food entirely from the canned version.

## The Science

- Cardiovascular Health Study: broiled/baked fish 3+/week = 49% lower MI death, 58% lower sudden cardiac death
- 4+ servings/week lowers heart attack risk by 22% (large observational studies)
- REDUCE-IT trial: high-dose EPA reduced major adverse CV events by 25% (Bhatt et al., 2019)
- Prospective cohort of 420,000: 2+ servings/week = 17% lower sudden cardiac death (Li et al., 2021)
- Cochrane review of 86 RCTs: moderate evidence for reduced coronary events from EPA/DHA (Abdelhamid et al., 2020)
